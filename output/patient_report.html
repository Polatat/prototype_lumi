RESULTS:
"A pathogenic mutation was identified in the HBA1 gene, resulting in a significant impact on the patient's hemoglobin production and overall hematologic function."
GENETIC INFORMATION

The HBA1 gene, located on chromosome 16, encodes for the alpha-globin protein. Alpha-globin is an essential component of the hemoglobin molecule, which is responsible for transporting oxygen throughout the body. The reported pathogenic mutation, chr16:23603525C>T, involves a single nucleotide change from cytosine to thymine in the HBA1 gene. This change results in a substitution of the amino acid arginine with cysteine at position 24 of the alpha-globin chain. The altered protein may impair the ability of hemoglobin to bind and release oxygen efficiently, potentially leading to anemia and other complications associated with reduced oxygen delivery to tissues.

A pathogenic mutation was identified in the BRCA1 gene, indicating a predisposition to hereditary breast and ovarian cancer.
GENETIC INFORMATION

The BRCA1 gene, located on chromosome 17, is responsible for encoding a protein that functions as a tumor suppressor, which plays a crucial role in repairing damaged DNA and preventing uncontrolled cell growth. The mutation identified in the BRCA1 gene (chr17:43091729G>A) is a single nucleotide substitution, where a guanine (G) is replaced with an adenine (A). This change may disrupt the protein's structure or function, potentially impairing its tumor suppressor activity and increasing the risk of developing cancer. The mutation in the BRCA1 gene can lead to an increased likelihood of developing breast and ovarian cancers, especially in women, due to the loss of the protective function of the BRCA1 protein.
